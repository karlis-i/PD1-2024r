Danny Brown, is an American rapper, singer and songwriter from Detroit, Michigan. He was described by MTV in 2011 as "one of rap's most unique figures in recent memory"

Born Daniel Dewan Sewell in Detroit on March 16, 1981. His talent for rhyming came at a very young age, as his mother would read Dr. Seuss books to him as a child, when he began to speak, he would talk in rhyme. His father was a house DJ who exposed him to all the music he would spin, as well as music from the likes of Roy Ayers, LL Cool J, Esham, and A Tribe Called Quest. For as long as he could remember, Brown had always wanted to be a rapper: "In kindergarten I'd say I wanted to be a rapper and people'd just laugh at me. 'That's a pretty funny job,' they'd say."

Albums

Quaranta

Quaranta is the sixth studio album released on November 17, 2023. Brown has called it his "most personal" album, on which he explores his experiences with pain, isolation, and hitting rock bottom

SCARING THE HOES

Scaring the Hoes (stylized in all caps) is a collaborative studio album with rapper JPEGMafia, it was released on March 24, 2023. Scaring the Hoes was released to widespread critical acclaim, who praised the chemistry between JPEGMafia and Brown, as well as the production of the album. Scaring the Hoes was primarily produced using the Roland SP-404SX.

uknowhatimsayin¿

U Know What I'm Sayin? (stylized as uknowhatimsayin¿) is the fifth studio album released on October 4, 2019. The album features guest appearances from Run the Jewels, Obongjayar, JPEGMafia and Blood Orange. Executive produced by Q-Tip, with contributions by Paul White, Flying Lotus, Thundercat, Standing on the Corner and JPEGMafia, the album received widespread critical acclaim.

Atrocity Exhibition

Atrocity Exhibition is the fourth studio album released on September 27, 2016. It is primarily produced by English producer Paul White, and features guest appearances from Kendrick Lamar, Ab-Soul, Earl Sweatshirt, B-Real, Kelela, and Petite Noir.

Old

Old is the third studio album released on October 8, 2013. The album is Brown's first project to be officially sold through music outlets and digital retailers, whereas his previous projects were self-released for free and made available online. Old includes production from American, British and Canadian record producers such as A-Trak, BadBadNotGood, Frank Dukes, Oh No, Paul White, Rustie and Skywlkr, among others. The album also features guest appearances from fellow artists such as Freddie Gibbs, ASAP Rocky, Schoolboy Q, Ab-Soul, Scrufizzer, Charli XCX and Purity Ring.

XXX

XXX (pronounced "triple x" or "30") is the second studio album released on August 15, 2011. The album's production was handled by Frank Dukes, Brandun DeShay, Skywlkr, Nick Speed, Quelle, Paul White, Squadda Bambino and DJ House Shoes. The album's only guest appearances come from Brown's Bruiser Brigade cohorts Chips and Dopehead.

The Hybrid

The Hybrid is the debut studio album by American rapper Danny Brown, released March 16, 2010. The album, which was preceded by a number of free mixtapes, was also released as a free download, by Washington, DC record label Rappers I Know.
