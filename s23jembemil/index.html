The Simpsons

The Simpsons is an American animated sitcom created by Matt Groening. The series is a satirical depiction of American life, epitomized by the Simpson family, which consists of Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, and Maggie. Set in the fictional town of Springfield, it caricatures society, Western culture, television, and the human condition.

Since its debut on December 17, 1989, 750 episodes of the show have been broadcast. It is the longest-running American sitcom and the longest-running American animated program.

Main Characters

Homer

Homer Jay Simpson is the bumbling father of the Simpson family. He works as a safety inspector at the Springfield Nuclear Power Plant.

Marge

Marjorie "Marge" Simpson is the loving mother of the Simpson family. She is known for her tall blue hair and her patience with Homer's antics.

Bart

Bartholomew "Bart" Simpson is the mischievous son of the Simpson family. He is known for his catchphrase, "Eat my shorts!"

Lisa

Lisa Simpson is the intelligent and saxophone-playing daughter of the Simpson family. She is known for her activism and love of learning.
